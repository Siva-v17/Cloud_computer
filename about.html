Our position

Infrastructure is not the product. What you build with it is.

We don't believe in platforms that make themselves the hero of your architecture diagram. STACKLY is designed to be the dependable, invisible layer underneath — so your team's attention stays on the product, not the plumbing.

That means fewer dashboards you have to babysit, incident reports that read like engineering, not marketing, and a pricing page that doesn't require a call to understand.

Timeline

Nine years, in the log.

  1. 2017

    First region online

    STACKLY launches with a single region and a compute API — built to replace the founders' own broken deploy process.

  2. 2019

    Object storage & managed Postgres

    Storage and data services ship, and the platform crosses 1,000 active accounts.

  3. 2021

    Global edge network

    Anycast routing goes live across 18 regions, cutting median latency by 60%.

  4. 2023

    99.99% SLA introduced

    Automated multi-region failover matures enough to back it with a contractual uptime guarantee.

  5. 2025

    Inference layer launches

    Managed GPU compute ships alongside the existing primitives, without a separate product to learn.

  6. 2026

    34 regions, 410PB managed

    Today STACKLY runs production infrastructure for teams from three-person startups to public companies.

How we operate

Four operating principles.

Ownership

Own the page

If it's our infrastructure, it's our incident. We don't route blame to a vendor's vendor.

Transparency

Default to transparent

Status pages, postmortems and pricing are public. No sales call required to understand what you're buying.

Reliability

Boring is a feature

We optimize for predictability over novelty. Your infrastructure shouldn't be the interesting part of your week.

Focus

Ship the primitive, not the wrapper

We build the underlying capability well rather than layering opinionated abstractions on top of it.
